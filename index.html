Hi there, my name is Torbjørn. I’m a 25 year old Norwegian who has been studying front-end development since the summer of 2020.

Before this began I worked at the operations department of Bergen’s port authority, and more recently I worked as a store employee at an electronics store, which is where I discovered how much I enjoy working with anything related to IT, despite never considering myself a very technical person.

A friend I hadn’t met in years had recently completed his studies in front-end development and, as I’d never heard much about it before, I was intrigued. He explained that it’s a growing field with plenty of job opportunities, and went into detail about what his job involved.

I immediately thought this could be something for me. So I looked into it further, and shortly afterwards I’d applied to study front-end development at Noroff Vocational School in Bergen.

I didn’t know much about web-development beforehand, but I was more motivated than I’d ever been in my life. I wanted to make this work. So I put in the hours and made no compromises with myself. I didn't have to force myself for long though, as I soon found out I really enjoy doing this. So much that I even started exploring and experimenting with subjects outside of our curriculum in my spare time.

Thanks to this mindset, I was able to learn more in this one year than any year before, and while my journey in web-development may have only just begun, I’m just as passionate as ever and I’m aiming to learn even more in the years to come.

GameBlog

HTML/CSS JavaScript

This is my latest project, and my first site using JavaScript. We were tasked with designing and developing a blogsite using WordPress as a headless CMS for our content.

View site View Github

Making the site

With the experience I had from the previous two websites, designing this one was a lot more intuitive and simple in comparison. The usual indecisiveness wasn’t nearly as present, as I was more confident in my choices.

Afterwards I built the site using HTML/CSS and fetched the blog posts I had on an external WordPress site through the REST API. I also conducted user-tests during and after development, and made several changes to my design based on their feedback.

This time around my workflow was a lot smoother than before, thanks to my experience from the previous projects. I also discovered many areas I could improve upon for future projects; mostly in terms of image optimization and JavaScript. But it’s a start, and I’m confident I’ll improve on these things as I learn and grow further.

In the future I hope to revisit this project to add a search functionality, expand each post with a comment section hooked up to the REST API, and make the contact form send messages directly to my email.

Science Museum

HTML/CSS JavaScript

This is my second project. This was a semester project where we were given a brief of a science museum and tasked to design and build a website specifically catered to the target audience.

View site View Github

Making the site

I was a lot more thorough in the groundwork here than on the previous project, as I now had a better idea of what I’d need and could prepare for it more elaborately.

I considered the psychological effects of my color palette and whether or not my typeface fit the theme of the website. I also went to great lengths to fine-tune my text for good readability, and was very particular in my choice of images.

In my code, I made sure that all of my components were modular so they could be re-used across the site. I used a proper naming convention to make my code intuitive and clean, and did my utmost to optimize performance.

I put my ‘finished’ web-page in the hands of as many people as I could find, and based on their feedback I made several changes. In doing so, I experienced how important user-testing is, and I made a note to myself to do so earlier in the future.

Overall, this project was magnitudes above my first on every facet. But I could still see much room for improvement. As this is a static HTML/CSS website it would benefit greatly from some dynamic interactions with JavaScript, and there’s a certain jankiness to the site as a whole that I’d want to clear up. Nonetheless, I’m proud with how my second website turned out in the end.

SquareEyes

HTML/CSS JavaScript

My first website ever. We were given a choice of briefs which we would design and build a website for, and I chose SquareEyes; an indie-film streaming service.

View site View Github

Making the site

This site was something we’d worked on throughout two courses. Designing the prototype was our final assignment for the design course, and developing it was our final assignment in the HTML/CSS course.

As we designed the page before we learned any HTML/CSS, I hadn’t designed it with development in mind. So there were certain parts I ended up improvising, which made my code rather dirty and rough.

But towards the end of the week spent making this website, I had discovered so many useful tricks and methods. I tried incorporating some of these methods into my code at the time, but could only do so much before the deadline.

In short, there were just a lot of growing pains all around, but it was still really enjoyable to make. The puzzle-solving of getting things the way you want them to be with CSS was almost addictive, so despite it not ending up the way I’d hoped, this project still has a special place in my heart due to all the things I learned while working on it.

I aim to revisit it some time in the coming months to clean up the messy code and add some additional functionality.
