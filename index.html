Hi there, my name is Torbjørn. I’m a 25 year old Norwegian who has been studying front-end development since the summer of 2020.

Before this began I worked at the operations department of Bergen’s port authority, and more recently I worked as a store employee at an electronics store, which is where I discovered how much I enjoy working with anything related to IT, despite never considering myself a very technical person.

A friend I hadn’t met in years had recently completed his studies in front-end development and, as I’d never heard much about it before, I was intrigued. He explained that it’s a growing field with plenty of job opportunities, and went into detail about what his job involved.

I immediately thought this could be something for me. So I looked into it further, and shortly afterwards I’d applied to study front-end development at Noroff Vocational School in Bergen.

I didn’t know much about web-development beforehand, but I was more motivated than I’d ever been in my life. I wanted to make this work. So I put in the hours and made no compromises with myself. I didn't have to force myself for long though, as I soon found out I really enjoy doing this. So much that I even started exploring and experimenting with subjects outside of our curriculum in my spare time.

Thanks to this mindset, I was able to learn more in this one year than any year before, and while my journey in web-development may have only just begun, I’m just as passionate as ever and I’m aiming to learn even more in the years to come.
